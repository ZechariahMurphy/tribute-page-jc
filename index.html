Julius Caesar

The man who made an empire.

Julius Caesar crossing the Rubicon River.

Gaius Julius Caesar, better known by his nomen gentilicium and cognomen Julius Caesar, was a Roman dictator, politician, and military general who played a critical role in the events that led to the demise of the Roman Republic and the rise of the Roman Empire.

Significant Life Events

  • 100 BC - Born into a political family. His uncle was a famous general named Marius
  • 73 BC - Elected Pontifex, he is well liked and quickly rises in leadership
  • 60 BC - At age 40, Julius Caesar is elected to the Consul. He worked closely with Pompey the Great and Crassus, two of Rome's leading figures
  • 58 BC - Invades other lands and conqueres Germany and Helvetti when they Gaul, or France
  • 49 BC - A Roman Civil War begins, Julius Caesar and Polpey the Great go to war against each other
  • 48 BC - Is victorious after a year-long battle, he emerged the victor from the Civil War with Pompay, who died in the end
  • 45 BC - Names himself dictator for life
  • 44 BC - Assassinated by political rivals on the steps of the senate in Rome
